Satellites

The satellite, humanity's mechanical sentinel in the sky, represents one of the most transformative leaps in our ability to understand and navigate the world. While the concept had been dreamed of for centuries from Isaac Newton's theoretical "cannonball" orbit to Jules Verne's fictional depictions it became a reality during the Cold War . The Space Age dawned on October 4, 1957, when the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1, the world's first artificial satellite . This beach-ball-sized sphere, which simply beeped as it circled the Earth, did more than just transmit radio pulses; it transmitted a shockwave through the West and ignited the intense space race between superpowers . The team behind this feat was led by chief designer Sergei Korolev, who turned theoretical rocket science into a tangible symbol of a new era . From those humble, beeping beginnings, satellites have evolved into the invisible backbone of modern civilization, enabling everything from global communications and GPS to weather forecasting and profound scientific discovery .
