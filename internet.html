Internet

The internet, a global network connecting billions of devices, stands as one of the most revolutionary inventions in human history. Its origins trace back to the 1960s during the Cold War, when the United States Department of Defense sought a communication system that could survive a nuclear attack. This project, led by the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA), resulted in ARPANET, the precursor to the modern internet. The first message was sent on October 29, 1969, between computers at UCLA and the Stanford Research Institute. The system attempted to transmit the word "login," but only the first two letters "lo" made it through before the network crashed a humble beginning for a technology that would transform the world. Key figures like computer scientists Vinton Cerf and Bob Kahn later developed the TCP/IP protocols, which became the standard language that allows different networks to communicate with each other. What began as a military experiment has evolved into an essential part of daily life, reshaping how we work, learn, communicate, and access information.
