Airplanes

The airplane represents one of humanity's oldest dreams finally realized the freedom to soar through the skies like birds. While many inventors experimented with flight, the credit for the first powered, controlled, and sustained flight belongs to brothers Wilbur and Orville Wright. On December 17, 1903, along the windswept shores of Kitty Hawk, North Carolina, Orville piloted the Wright Flyer for twelve seconds, covering just 120 feet. It was a modest beginning for a technology that would shrink our planet from weeks of travel to mere hours. The Wright brothers, bicycle mechanics from Ohio, succeeded where others failed because they understood that flight required not just powerful engines but also mastery of control. They built the first practical aircraft by solving the problem of balance, developing a system of wing warping and movable rudders that allowed pilots to steer. From that first fragile flight, aviation evolved rapidly through world wars and commercial revolutions, until crossing oceans became routine and visiting other continents became possible in a single day.
